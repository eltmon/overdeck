Backlog detail — Plan → Release, now with the AI Objection (5th state)

Same row as before, but planning now has two possible outcomes. When asked to plan, the AI first judges the item. If it's fine → Planned → operator Releases (mockup v1). If the AI judges it doesn't make sense, would make the product worse, or has been superseded, it does not silently plan — it raises a Held for Review / AI Objection with a written explanation. The operator can Override the objection (force it forward to Planned) or accept it & park. Left panel is the objection case (live — try Override / Park). Right panel is the happy path (live — Plan → Release).

What the Objection is for

The problemAuto-planning will dutifully plan anything you mark — even work that's stale, redundant, or net-negative. The cost shows up later as a merged regression or wasted agent time.
The gateGive the planning AI a way to say "no, and here's why" before work starts — surfaced to you, with a written case and a one-click override. You stay in control; the AI just refuses to plan silently.
