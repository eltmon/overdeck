Backlog Pickup Forecast · real data · modeled at 5 concurrent agents

Instead of arrows = raw dependencies, this models the order the Flywheel will actually pick up work — applying the real eligibility rules (gate≠blocked · not already running · must have a vBRIEF spec or PRD draft) and processing in rank order, 5 at a time. Three ways to read it. All numbers are live from /api/backlog/sequence.
1 Pickup Waves — "what gets picked next, 5 at a time"
Each wave is the next batch of 5 eligible issues in rank order. The bold arrows are the pickup sequence: when a wave's slots free up, the next wave starts. "In flight now" = the 7 issues currently running (the pulsing nodes on the graph).
2 Agent-Lane Forecast — "when, and where's the long pole"
5 agent lanes, time → right (block width ∝ effort: XS…XL). Each ready issue drops into the first lane that frees up, in pickup order — a realistic parallel forecast. The makespan shows how long 5 agents take to clear the ready queue (≈ 6 effort-units).
critical high medium block width = estimated effort
3 The Planning Bottleneck — "the highest-leverage move the graph reveals"
The forecast exposes something a dependency graph can't: your top-ranked criticals are not in the pickup queue at all — they have no spec or PRD, so auto-pickup skips them. Planning them is what actually unblocks priority throughput.
