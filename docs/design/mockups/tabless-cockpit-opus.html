Overview→ “Now” section (open by default) + the header/lane/rail already carry status Code→ “Code” section (open): CI + changed-files tree + diff (#7) PRD / Plan→ “Plan” section (collapsed) Discussion→ “Discussion” section (collapsed) Timeline→ “Timeline” section (collapsed) Costs→ “Costs” section (collapsed; total always in the header) Artifacts→ “Artifacts” section (collapsed) Conversation→ sticky composer at the center bottom + select an agent for its conversation Files / Terminal / Web→ header tool-buttons → deck panes Tab navigation→ scrollspy gutter (jump + current-position), content all present

The trade vs. tabs — decide before I build

  • Win: everything is one screen, nothing hidden behind a click; the scrollspy shows where you are; deep stuff collapses so it’s not overwhelming.
  • Cost: a long scroll vs. instant tab random-access. The scrollspy mitigates it, and Now+Code (the 90% case) are open at the top.
  • 1. Default-open sections: Now + Code open, the rest collapsed (as shown). Or remember per-issue what you last expanded?
  • 2. Scrollspy gutter on the center’s left (shown) — or a sticky top jump-bar, or none (pure scroll)?
  • 3. The sticky composer at the bottom for issue-level launch/message — keep it, or only ever talk to agents by selecting them in the lane?
