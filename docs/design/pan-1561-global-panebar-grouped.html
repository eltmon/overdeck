PAN-1561 · Global PaneBar — Variant B

Same outermost global bar as Variant A, but with the three things that keep a cross-workspace bar navigable at volume: workspace grouping (collapsible clusters), overflow, and a ⌘P quick-switcher. Group headers preview the #type tag idea. Draft for discussion.

1 · Grouped, with overflow + quick-switch

20 open panes read as a handful of workspace clusters, each headed by its WS · #type · count. Collapse a cluster to a chip; overflow folds the rest; ⌘P fuzzy-finds any pane instantly.

Why this beats a flat strip at volume: Variant A's flat bar is great at 2–3 workspaces and cramped past ~10 tabs. Grouping makes 20 panes read as 4 clusters; collapse + overflow keep the bar short; ⌘P makes tab count irrelevant for reaching anything. The #type tag (#feature / #bug / #explore) adds a second axis you can scan and, later, filter by.
