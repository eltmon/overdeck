UAT candidate naming — likely several per day, so the date alone isn't unique

uat/pan-2026-06-09-1430Date + time (HH:MM). Unique, sorts chronologically, machine-clean. Downside: a mouthful to say out loud.
uat/pan-otter-0609 ✓Recommended. Random codename + short date. Memorable and easy to refer to verbally ("ship the otter candidate"), still dated. Needs a small wordlist; collision-checked.
uat/pan-2026-06-09-a / -bDate + letter. Clean, but letters reset daily so "candidate B" is ambiguous when you look back days later.
uat/pan-c42Monotonic counter. Shortest + globally unique + sortable, but opaque — tells you nothing about when or what.
uat/pan-1685+1455Names its contents (lead issues). Self-describing, but grows long and changes if the bundle changes.
My pick: codename + date — you'll be saying these names to me and to each other; "the otter candidate" beats "uat-2026-06-09-1430". If you'd rather it be machine-sortable above all, date+time is the safe second.
