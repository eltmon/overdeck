# PAN-2174 — bug(flywheel): intermittently idles instead of ticking

**Issue:** https://github.com/eltmon/overdeck/issues/2174

## Glossary

- **Flywheel orchestrator** — the singleton "Fix-All Flywheel" agent (`flywheel-orchestrator`, role `flywheel`) that inventories the pipeline, launches/plans work, and emits a status snapshot every tick. Driven by `roles/flywheel.md`.
- **Tick** — one full pass: inventory → diagnose → suggest → launch → emit-status. Should complete in under a minute.
- **ScheduleWakeup** — the claude-code harness tool the flywheel calls at the end of a tick (`delaySeconds: 1000`) to schedule its own next tick. 1000s ≈ 16.7 min, under the 20-min periodic-sweep watchdog.
- **Deacon idle patrol / stuck-remediation** — the deacon's per-minute patrol (`src/lib/cloister/stuck-remediation.ts`) that detects alive-but-idle agents and nudges or relaunches them. For the flywheel it runs through `evaluateFlywheelOrchestrator()`.
- **Stage 1 / 2 / 3** — escalating remediation stages keyed off idle minutes. Stage 1 = poke message, Stage 2 = escalated message, Stage 3 = terminal action (for the flywheel: kill+fresh-launch the run, capped; PAN-2160).
- **Relaunch** — the deacon kills the wedged orchestrator's tmux session and starts a fresh flywheel run via `resumeFlywheel()`, capped at `FLYWHEEL_MAX_RESPAWNS = 3`.

## Problem

Observed 2026-06-29 (RUN-37, opus/claude-code): the flywheel ticked once, asked a cadence question (AskUserQuestion), then sat idle for 46 minutes at the prompt — violating `roles/flywheel.md:111` (*never block on the operator*) and `roles/flywheel.md:121` (*ScheduleWakeup every tick*). The failure is intermittent: RUN-38 ticked fine. Root cause: opus/claude-code **sometimes drops the self-schedule (ScheduleWakeup) or emits a blocking question**, leaving the live session idle indefinitely.

Recovery exists today but is weak:

1. The deacon idle nudge (stage 1 at 20 min, stage 2 at 45 min) sends a message that says *"Emit a current status … or run `pan flywheel pause`"* — it tells the agent to **emit a snapshot or pause**, NOT to **run a real tick**. An orchestrator that re-emits a stale snapshot and goes idle again has technically "responded" without resuming pipeline work.
2. The terminal relaunch (stage 3) fires at the **shared 90-minute** threshold (`stuck_remediation.stage3_minutes`). For a sub-minute-tick singleton that is the pipeline's last stand, 90 minutes of dead air is far too late.

This bug is lower priority than the throughput issue because recovery does eventually happen — but the recovery latency (up to 90 min) and the weak nudge semantics make the flywheel unreliable.

## Proposal

Two coordinated, recovery-side changes (the harness-level "never drop ScheduleWakeup" fix is out of scope — see NonGoals):

### (a) Make the deacon idle-nudge a hard "run a FULL tick now" trigger

Rewrite the flywheel stage-1 and stage-2 nudge messages so they direct the orchestrator to **run a complete tick immediately** (inventory → diagnose → suggest → launch → emit-status) and then re-arm `ScheduleWakeup(1000s)` — explicitly forbidding "ask a question and wait" and "just emit a stale snapshot". This converts the nudge from a status-prompt into a real pipeline-advancing trigger.

### (b) Flywheel-specific, tighter relaunch threshold (~28 min, not 90)

Add flywheel-specific stage thresholds to `StuckRemediationConfig` so the flywheel relaunches at **28 min** instead of the shared 90 min, without changing the work-agent thresholds.

**Chosen default values (autoDecision):**

| Stage | Work-agent (shared, unchanged) | Flywheel (new) | Rationale |
|-------|-------------------------------|----------------|-----------|
| stage1 (poke / full-tick nudge) | 20 | **20** | A healthy flywheel self-wakes at 1000s ≈ 16.7 min, so 20 min is the first point at which a missed/dropped wake is provable. Lowering it would pre-empt healthy `ScheduleWakeup` sleeps and cause spurious nudges. |
| stage2 (escalated full-tick nudge) | 45 | **24** | ~4 min after stage 1 — a tick is sub-minute, so 4 min is enough for the stage-1 nudge to take effect before escalating. |
| stage3 (relaunch) | 90 | **28** | Within the issue's stated 25-30 min target. Fresh-launch (new session) is the reliable recovery for a wedged-at-AskUserQuestion session that ignores pasted nudges. |

New fields: `flywheel_stage1_minutes` (20), `flywheel_stage2_minutes` (24), `flywheel_stage3_minutes` (28).

## Requirements

- **FR-1** — The flywheel stage-1 and stage-2 deacon nudge messages MUST instruct the orchestrator to run a full tick now (inventory→diagnose→suggest→launch→emit-status) and re-arm `ScheduleWakeup(1000s)`, and MUST NOT tell it merely to emit a status or pause as the primary instruction.
- **FR-2** — The flywheel relaunch (stage 3) MUST fire at the flywheel-specific threshold (default 28 min), independent of the shared `stage3_minutes` used by work agents.
- **FR-3** — `StuckRemediationConfig` MUST expose `flywheel_stage1_minutes`, `flywheel_stage2_minutes`, `flywheel_stage3_minutes`, defaulting to 20/24/28, overridable via TOML deep-merge.
- **FR-4** — Work-agent stuck-remediation thresholds (20/45/90) and behavior MUST be unchanged.
- **NFR-1** — Stage 1 MUST NOT fire before 20 min idle, so healthy `ScheduleWakeup(1000s ≈ 16.7 min)` self-wakes are not pre-empted.
- **NFR-2** — The relaunch cap (`FLYWHEEL_MAX_RESPAWNS = 3`) and pause+troubled escalation on cap-exhaustion MUST be preserved.

## NonGoals

- Do NOT attempt a harness-level fix that makes opus/claude-code "never drop ScheduleWakeup" or "never emit a blocking question" — this issue is recovery-side hardening only.
- Do NOT change the work-agent stage thresholds (20/45/90) or any non-flywheel remediation behavior.
- Do NOT change the flywheel's ScheduleWakeup interval (stays 1000s).
- Do NOT remove or raise the relaunch cap (`FLYWHEEL_MAX_RESPAWNS = 3`) or the pause+troubled terminal escalation.
- Do NOT programmatically auto-answer or dismiss the orchestrator's AskUserQuestion modal — relaunch (fresh session) is the recovery.
- Do NOT lower stage 1 below 20 min (would pre-empt healthy self-wakes).
